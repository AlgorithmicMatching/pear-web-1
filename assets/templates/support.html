What are the main screens of the app?

The app is very simple. There is a settings page where you set your age/location restrictions, your notification settings, and so on. There is a profile page where you can build your profile. The core of the app is the comparison screen, where you are showed two users at a time and are asked to prefer one of them. Finally, there is the match screen that contains all your matches. Go on the match page to start chatting with your matches.

How does the comparison screen work?

We will show you two profiles at the time, and we expect you to decide with each profile which you prefer among the two users. To express the preference for a user, click on the Pear logo on the user’s picture! Your preferences will remain private and will never be disclosed to anybody, except in an anonymous way.

Why do I keep seeing the same profiles in the comparison screen?

You never get to see the same couple of profiles twice, but you may see the same profile multiple times. In fact, as you keep comparing other users, our algorithm will keep track of your preferences, basically ranking all previously seen users from the one you like most to the one you like least. Therefore, when you are shown a new user, in order to determine his or her ranking, the algorithm needs to compare this person with people you have already ranked.

Why are my matches divided between “Stable” and “Expiring”?

A match is a match, whether it is stable or expiring. However, there are two main differences between stable and expiring matches. Firstly, while stable matches are stable, your expiring matches will expire after a predetermined period of time! Secondly, while there is a limited number of stable matches you can have at each point in time, the number of expiring matches is not bounded. Initially all new matches are stable, but then some (or all of them) can be replaced by other stable matches and turn into expiring matches.
